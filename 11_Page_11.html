It felt similar to tickling, but smelled different. People often overlook the fact that each touch has its own smell. Most tickles have a smell of iron. When one touches my hair, it smells sandalwood. This time, there was a smell that I had never smelled before. It smelled like a mixture of ripe oranges, musk, and plumeria. I began to move in search of the source of smell. It was clearly the smell of climbing up the spine from the point slightly below the navel. But every time I tried to reach the source, the boy took off his hand, and the smell soon disappeared into the air. My pleasure was longing for reason.
